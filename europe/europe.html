London had a lot of history to it which can be seen through walking through the parks and riverwalks. Buckingham Palace itself is something to look at. We took one of the river cruises and passed by Big Ben, the London Flyer and the London bridge.

Paris was full of life, filled with its rich history and well known landmarks. Going up the Eiffel Tower via elevator was exciting to experience, but standing at the top and looking at the distance of buildings is spectacular. There was history everywhere from the Lourve to the Arc de Triomphe. We also visited Mon Marte which had restaraunts and a church that overlooks the city. We even passed by the famous Moulin Rouge and church of Notre Dame.

Florence was my favorite city. Italian food, famous buildings, and gelato was what made the trip. Every corner there was pretty much a gelato store. We did see the statue of David and tombs of influencial people such as Michelangelo, Galileo, and Machiavelli. The Duomo, a church, is at the central part of the city and is the tallest building there. It was amazing looking up and seeing the ceilings painted and preserved for more than 400 years.

Rome had iconic attractions and locations. Home to the Vatican, we were able to enter St. Paul's Basillica and see tombs of prior popes. Behind the Vatican also had the museum which housed the Sistene chapel. Outside of the Vatican though, we visited the Colloseum, Trevi fountain, and the one of the first Catholic churches. Did I mention more gelato?

Barcelona felt relaxed. Other than the main street that has shopping, there were many buildings and architecture make it unique. Gaudi's artistic vision is portrayed in many of the buildings there. The Sagrada Familia stood out as a masterpiece still being built after Gaudi's death almost a hundred years later. Casa Mila can be seen on the main shopping street on a corner. It was also a stressful time as we were there during the Catalan independence movement. Other than that, eating Paella and finding even more Gelato places were great!
